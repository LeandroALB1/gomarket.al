# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Albanian small-business owners and local shop sellers (fashion, electronics, handmade, home, food, beauty, services). Many are non-technical, mobile-first, evaluating whether to trust a new marketplace before it launches. Context: browsing on a phone, deciding in seconds whether GoMarket is legitimate and worth handing over their email — and eventually their sales.

## Product Purpose

GoMarket is a pre-launch waitlist landing page for a modern marketplace that lets Albanian businesses sell online with no website and no technical skill. The page's job is to communicate the promise, build trust, and convert visitors into waitlist signups. Success = a qualified business signs up and believes the product is real and worth waiting for.

## Brand Personality

Warm, local, approachable, confident. Speaks Albanian to Albanian businesses. Optimistic about local commerce ("Bërë për Shqipërinë"). Should feel like a credible, founder-led product on the verge of launch — not a faceless corporate platform, not a hype-machine.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI SaaS template.** No gradient text, no eyebrow above every section, no identical icon-card grid, no hero-metric stat template as default scaffolding. The current page leans on several of these.
- **Cheap / scammy.** Must read as trustworthy to a business owner about to share contact info and later money. No fake-feeling social proof, no overstated claims, no template polish that signals "anyone could spin this up."

## Design Principles

1. **Trust before delight.** Every animation and gradient must earn its place against the goal of looking legitimate to a cautious local business.
2. **Speak the user's language, literally.** Albanian copy, local references, plain words over marketing jargon.
3. **Mobile-first reality.** The audience is on phones; the phone-zone, tap targets, and signup flow must be flawless small.
4. **Honest social proof.** Counts and claims should be real or clearly framed, never inflated to manipulate.
5. **One job per page.** Drive the waitlist signup; everything else supports that single conversion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (watch muted green-gray on tinted near-white), large text ≥3:1, placeholders ≥4.5:1. Full keyboard operability for the form and admin modal, visible focus states. `prefers-reduced-motion` must neutralize the aurora, orbs, parallax, and background shift (page is heavily animated).
